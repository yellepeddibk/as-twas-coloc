% ── Introduction ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\begin{block}{Introduction}
\justifying
\textbf{Ankylosing spondylitis (AS)} is a chronic inflammatory arthritis of
the spine and sacroiliac joints affecting approximately 0.1--0.5\% of the
population, with onset typically before age 30. While genome-wide association
studies (GWAS) have identified over 100 risk loci, the majority fall in
non-coding regions, making it difficult to pinpoint causal genes and
actionable drug targets.

\vskip0.6em
\textbf{Transcriptome-wide association studies (TWAS)} integrate GWAS with
gene expression prediction models to identify genes whose genetically
regulated expression is associated with disease risk. However, TWAS hits can
arise from \textit{linkage disequilibrium (LD) contamination} rather than
true shared causal variants.

\vskip0.6em
\textbf{Bayesian colocalization} (coloc) tests whether a GWAS signal and an
eQTL signal share the same causal variant, distinguishing genuine
tissue-specific regulatory effects from LD artifacts.
\end{block}

% ── Key Question ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\begin{alertblock}{Key Question}
\centering\large
Which genes have genetically regulated expression that \textbf{causally
colocalizes} with AS risk variants, and in which tissues?
\end{alertblock}

% ── TWAS Results ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\begin{block}{TWAS Results}
\justifying
S-PrediXcan tested \textbf{2,316 gene-tissue pairs} across 8 GTEx v8
tissues. After Benjamini--Hochberg FDR correction ($\alpha = 0.05$),
\textbf{248 significant associations} were identified, spanning genes on
multiple chromosomes.

\vskip0.5em
\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/manhattan_twas.png}
\captionof{figure}{Manhattan-style plot of TWAS $-\!\log_{10}(p)$ across 8
tissues. Top gene symbols annotated. Dashed red line: FDR $< 0.05$.}
\end{center}

\vskip0.3em
\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.72\linewidth]{figures/qqplot_twas.png}
\captionof{figure}{QQ-plot showing strong departure from the null,
consistent with polygenic architecture. Grey band: 95\% CI.}
\end{center}
\end{block}

% ── Colocalization Results ───────────────────────────────────────────────────
\begin{block}{Colocalization Results}
\justifying
Of the 248 TWAS hits, Bayesian colocalization (coloc.abf) identified
\textbf{35 gene-tissue pairs (18 unique genes)} with PP4 $\geq$ 0.70,
confirming a shared causal variant between GWAS and eQTL signals.

\vskip0.5em
\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.72\linewidth]{figures/coloc_pp4_vs_pp3pp4.png}
\captionof{figure}{PP4 vs.\ locus power (PP3+PP4). Points in the shaded
upper-right quadrant are colocalization-confirmed hits.}
\end{center}
\end{block}

% ── Discussion ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\begin{block}{Discussion}
\justifying
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textit{ERAP1} (aminopeptidase involved in MHC-I antigen processing)
    is a well-established AS gene, validating our pipeline. Its strong
    colocalization in both Whole Blood and Small Intestine highlights
    tissue-specific regulatory mechanisms.
  \item Novel candidates such as \textit{GPR25}, \textit{CCDC116}, and
    \textit{NPIPB7} warrant experimental follow-up as potential
    tissue-specific drug targets.
  \item \textit{CARD9} and \textit{FAS} implicate innate immune and
    apoptotic pathways beyond the canonical HLA-B27 axis.
  \item Colocalization filtering reduced 248 TWAS hits to 35 confirmed
    gene-tissue pairs, eliminating LD-driven false positives.
\end{itemize}
\end{block}

% ── Conclusion ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\begin{block}{Conclusion}
\justifying
Integrating S-PrediXcan TWAS with Bayesian colocalization across 8 tissues
identified \textbf{18 unique genes} with strong evidence of shared causal
variants with AS risk loci. This two-stage approach provides a principled
filter against LD contamination and prioritizes tissue-specific regulatory
targets for therapeutic development.
\end{block}

% ── Future Directions ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
